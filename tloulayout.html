A Game That Will Touch Your Heart

Unlike most zombie games, The Last of Us focuses not only on death, blood, and destruction. The game’s story is actually one of the main elements that make it so successful and beloved by many. Players get to know the main characters — Joel and Ellie — two people who have formed an unlikely yet strong partnership. They come from completely different walks of life: Joel, a long-time smuggler already accustomed to violence and death, and Ellie, still young and naïve. The dangerous quest begins when Joel is tasked with escorting Ellie to the Fireflies, a group trying to find a cure for the zombie infection. What makes Ellie so special is her well-known immunity to it, and this is why Joel must be able to bring her to the Fireflies. The exhausting journey is filled with bloodshed and difficult situations that only strengthen the bond between Ellie and Joel. While at first Joel claims he doesn’t like Ellie at all, this completely changes in the second part of the game. In fact, Joel’s change of heart will be a decisive factor in how the game ends. There will be no spoilers about what happens in the finale, but if there’s one thing players should know, it’s that The Last of Us makes an effort to break away from clichés and challenge people’s expectations.

The Journey Ahead

The Last of Us offers players a highly realistic depiction of what a zombie apocalypse might look like. Brutal actions in this game are, of course, inevitable, as you will face zombies moving at lightning speed with only faint traces of human flesh left. The conflict between the game’s characters will also force players to confront them. There are paranoid street gangs and despicable soldiers who are more than willing to shoot another person rather than risk becoming infected. In short, the journey to safely deliver Ellie to the Fireflies will be far from a walk in the park , so players should expect plenty of struggle and violence in this game.
